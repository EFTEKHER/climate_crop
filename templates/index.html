View Analysis

Our model leverages machine learning to predict crop yields based on climate factors such as rainfall, temperature, and pesticide use. This enhances resource optimization, helps farmers adapt to changing climates, and bolsters food security. By providing actionable insights, it supports sustainable agricultural practices and informs policymakers on effective climate action strategies, contributing to a more resilient agriculture system in the face of climate change. Our Crop Yield Prediction Model leverages advanced machine learning algorithms to forecast crop yields by analyzing various environmental and agricultural factors such as average rainfall, pesticide usage, and temperature. The model employs five algorithms: Decision Tree, Random Forest, XGBoost, Extra Trees, and AdaBoost, each offering different strengths in prediction accuracy and error minimization. Performance metrics, including the R2 Score (measuring prediction accuracy) and Mean Absolute Error (MAE, indicating the average prediction error), ensure the model's reliability.Users can easily input the type of crop, the year, and relevant environmental data to generate predictions. For example, by entering the crop type (e.g., Maize), the year (e.g., 2023), average rainfall, pesticide usage, and average temperature, the model will predict the crop yield for that year. This tool is designed to support farmers and agricultural planners in making informed, data-driven decisions to optimize crop yields and improve agricultural productivity. The model also provides visualizations, such as histograms, box plots, and correlation matrices, to help users understand the data and the model's performance better.
